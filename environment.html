Phosphorus Pollution

Even low concentrations of phosphorus can accelerate algae growth, reduce oxygen levels in water, and cause aquatic life to suffocate and die. Unlike agricultural runoff, municipal emissions are a controllable source of pollution.

Eutrophication

Excessive nutrients can turn clear water into murky, oxygen-depleted areas. This leads to fish deaths, toxic algal blooms, and food chain disruptions. Algal blooms produce toxins that are harmful to humans, pets, and livestock.
