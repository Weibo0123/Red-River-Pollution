Combined Sewer Overflows

Much of Winnipeg's oldest infrastructure uses a combined sewer system — where rainwater and sewage share the same pipes. During heavy rain or snowmelt, the system overflows, dumping raw or partially treated sewage directly into the Red River.

SOURCE: CITY OF WINNIPEG WATER AND WASTE DEPARTMENT

SYSTEM NOTE

Why the system is not fixed

The issue persists less as a single failure than as a chain of structural conditions: cost, age, invisibility, and accepted risk.

COST

Capital cost

Separating combined sewer infrastructure requires multi-billion dollar reconstruction. Projects compete with housing, transit, and road maintenance budgets.

INERTIA

System inertia

The network was built over decades. Full replacement would require decades more disruption across residential and industrial zones.

VISIBILITY

Invisibility

The infrastructure is underground. Failures are not continuously visible, reducing sustained public pressure.

RISK

Accepted risk

Overflow events are treated as statistically acceptable under current design standards during extreme weather conditions.
